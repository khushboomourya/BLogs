From industrial to commercial settings, hand safety in the workplace is paramount. Our hands are essential for performing everyday tasks, and protecting them is crucial to ensure worker health and productivity. One of the most effective ways to maintain hand safety at work is by using the correct hand protection equipment, like safety gloves. From protecting workers against chemical burns to cuts, or reducing the risk of contamination, safety gloves play a significant role in preventing workplace injuries.

In this blog, we have explored the top 5 industries where hand protection is essential.

Top 5 Industries Where Safety Gloves are Essential

Manufacturing and Engineering: In these industries, workers handle sharp tools, heavy materials, and heated surfaces daily. They are susceptible to injuries like cuts, abrasions, and burns. Hence, hand safety measures are crucial in the manufacturing and engineering industries.

  1. Gloves Used
    • Supported/Mechanical Resistance Gloves
    • Multi-Purpose Heavy-Duty Gloves
    • Cut and Heat Protection Gloves
    • Mechanical and Cut-Resistance Gloves
  2. Risks Prevented
    • Sharp tool injuries while assembling machinery.
    • Exposure to heat during metal fabrication.
    • Possibilities of punctures and abrasions during part handling.

Healthcare and Medical: Medical professionals are mostly exposed to pathogens, bloodborne contaminants, and other biohazards. Using disposable gloves helps prevent cross-contamination, protecting both patients and staff.

  1. Gloves Used
    • Disposable Gloves (Nitrile)
  2. Risks Prevented
    • Viral and bacterial infections.
    • Chemical exposure caused by disinfectants.
    • Skin irritation from cleaning solutions.

Construction: High-risk tasks such as lifting heavy equipment, electrical work, and handling sharp objects or rough materials are common in the construction sector. To minimize the risk of injuries, it is crucial to use proper hand protection equipment.

  1. Gloves Used
    • Cut and Impact Protection Gloves
    • Impact Resistant Gloves
    • Insulated Gloves
  2. Risks Prevented
    • Injuries from falling tools.
    • Cuts from sharp building materials.
    • Electric shocks during wiring tasks.

Chemical and Pharmaceutical Industry: Working in labs and pharmaceutical plants involves handling corrosive substances and hazardous chemicals. Proper hand safety can protect employees from serious skin injuries or toxic exposure.

  1. Gloves Used
    • Chemical Resistant Gloves (Nitrile, Neoprene, PVC)
    • Rubber Gloves
    • Arm Protection Sleeves
  2. Risks Prevented
    • Burns from chemicals.
    • Skin irritation from solvents.
    • Toxic exposure during spills.

Food Processing: To maintain hygiene and prevent injuries, hand safety in food handling is essential. Workers in the food industry often deal with sharp knives, slicers, and high-speed machinery. This exposure makes them susceptible to cross-contamination and cuts.

  1. Gloves Used
    • Food-Handling Gloves
    • Cut-Resistant Gloves
  2. Risks Prevented
    • Contamination of food products.
    • Bacterial transmission.
    • Injuries from knives during slicing or packaging.

Are You Using the Right Safety Gloves? Reasons Why Choosing the Right Gloves Matters

Not all safety gloves offer the same specifications. A glove that works perfectly in a healthcare setting might not be ideal for a chemical handling or metal-cutting setup. Using the wrong glove can lead to serious injuries, infections, or contamination.

Here are some key factors to consider while buying safety gloves:

  • Material: Ensure you select the material of the glove depending on the task you will be performing. There are different options, such as nitrile, latex, leather, etc, to choose from.
  • Durability: Consider how long the gloves will endure in high-stress work environments.
  • Fit: Ill-fitted gloves can be risky as they reduce grip and impact the wearerâ€™s comfort.
  • Resistance: Ensure that the glove you choose offers protection against chemicals, heat, or cuts.
  • Expiration: Do not use a safety glove past its expiration date.
  • Wear and Tear: Check if the gloves you pick have signs of physical damage, such as tears, discoloration, or swelling.
