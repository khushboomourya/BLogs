Functions of Lanyards in Construction

Safety lanyards work as a link between a worker’s body harness and a secure anchorage point. These can limit movement, place workers in a safe position, or arrest falls during any accident. When properly used as part of a fall protection system, lanyards help minimize the risk of serious injuries or fatalities.

Workers on construction sites may require access to roofs, scaffolds, and towers. In these situations, lanyards become essential. They help maintain balance while working, minimize fall distance, and provide a stop during a fall. They safeguard workers against fatal risks.

Types of Lanyards in Construction

There are different types of lanyards, each for a specific purpose within the larger umbrella of fall protection equipment. Below are the common types of safety lanyards used in construction sites:

  • Work Positioning Lanyards: These lanyards help hold the worker in a secure position, allowing hands-free operation (For example, PN247). Work positioning lanyards are used when workers need to perform tasks while being suspended or standing on an elevated surface without the risk of falling. They are ideal for tower climbing or scaffold work and are used with a body harness and a backup fall arrest gear. Work positioning lanyards enable precise positioning and stability for tasks that require both hands.
  • Restraint Lanyards: These types of lanyards protect workers from facing a fall hazard in the first place, for example, PN204. They restrain the movement rather than arrest a fall. This keeps the worker within a safety zone. Restraint lanyards are best suited for flat roofs or platforms with open edges. Unlike other lanyards, these are preventive safety lanyards—minimalist in design but highly effective at preventing accidents.

    ➢ Restraint Forked Lanyards: A forked lanyard, also known as a twin-leg lanyard, ensures continuous fall protection when working at heights. It enables 100% tie-off, allowing users to remain securely connected while transitioning between anchor points and navigating around structures safely.

  • Energy Absorbing Lanyards: Also known as shock-absorbing lanyards, energy-absorbing lanyards are important for fall arrest situations (For example, PN371). These lanyards help reduce the impact force on the body upon a fall by using a deceleration device. Energy-absorbing lanyards must be used with compatible fall arrest gear such as full-body harnesses and certified anchor points.

    ➢ Forked Lanyard with Energy Absorber: Forked lanyards with energy absorbers are available in twisted rope, Kernmantle rope, and 44mm polyester webbing.

Importance of Lanyards in Construction Safety

Using lanyards as part of a fall protection equipment system is not simply a safety practice; it is a legal and regulatory requirement. Since construction workers are often subject to operating at heights, where a minor slip can cause serious injury or be life-threatening, lanyards play a significant role.

Here is why lanyards are an indispensable safety gear:

  • Helps Prevent Fatalities: By attaching the harness to an anchor point, safety lanyards prevent workers from falling off elevated platforms, scaffolds, or ladders, thus reducing the risk of serious injury or fatality.
  • Safety Regulations Compliant: Using certified fall protection gear like lanyards ensures compliance with occupational health and safety regulations. With stricter laws in place, companies are required to use appropriate fall protection equipment on construction sites.
  • Improves Productivity and Confidence: Workers, knowing they are protected by reliable fall arrest gear, can perform tasks at heights with greater focus.
  • Adapts to Various Work Conditions: From working on a sharp-edged roof to an inclined roof, or any high platform, there is a specific lanyard to match each work scenario. Their versatility makes them a staple in working height safety equipment.
  • Minimizes Liability and Financial Risk: Workplace accidents lead to costly lawsuits and insurance claims. Lanyards and other fall protection systems reduce such risks significantly by safeguarding the workers.

Using the right working height safety equipment, like lanyards, protects lives and reduces liability, project delays, and potential financial loss for construction companies.
