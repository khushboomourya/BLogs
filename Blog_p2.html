Common Hazards in Industrial and Construction Sites

There are a variety of risks involved in harsh environments, including:

  • Flying particles from metal and woodwork.
  • Dust and sand in construction zones.
  • Sparks and flashes during welding or grinding.
  • Chemical splashes in laboratories and manufacturing units.
  • UV and infrared radiation during electrical or welding tasks.

Reliable, high-impact eye protection is crucial in activities like drilling, sawing, sanding, and welding. Lack of proper eyewear in these scenarios often leads to eye strain, injuries, and long-term vision loss.

Tips for Choosing the Right KARAM Eyewear

Choosing the right eyewear depends on:

  • Work Environment: Consider the main hazards like dust, chemicals, sparks, or radiation.
  • Comfort and Fit: Choose adjustable frames and nose bridges for more comfort during extended wear.
  • Lens Type: Choose clear lenses for indoor or low-light use. Choose tinted or mirrored lenses for outdoor use and UV protection. Choose anti-fog lenses for high-humidity zones or when wearing face masks.

Maintenance and Care for Long-Lasting Protection

To maintain your safety eyewear,

  • Clean regularly using a soft microfiber cloth and non-abrasive solution.
  • Store properly in a protective case to avoid scratches and impact.
  • Inspect regularly for cracks, scratches, or distortion.
  • If the lenses appear damaged or visibility gets impaired, replace them immediately.
