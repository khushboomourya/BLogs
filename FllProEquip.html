Falling from a height is one of the leading causes of workplace accidents and fatalities in India. The 2023 report by the British Safety Council cites that 40 workers were killed by falls, with 24 of the accidents happening in the construction sector. Statistics like these highlight the necessity for proper fall protection strategies in different industries.

What is Fall Protection?

Fall protection encompasses several strategies and equipment designed to prevent or arrest falls in the workplace. It includes full-body harnesses, fall arrest systems, lanyards, and more.

However, it is essential to distinguish between:

  • Fall Prevention: Encompasses all the precautions that eliminate the risk of falls.
  • Fall Arrest: Involves equipment that stops a fall in progress, such as harnesses and lanyards.

Understanding and implementing both aspects are crucial for worker safety.

Common Workplace Fall Hazards

Identifying and eliminating fall risks are crucial to ensuring workplace safety. Some common scenarios include:

  • Elevated Surfaces: This involves work done on roofs, scaffolding, or ladders without adequate safety precautions.
  • Unprotected Openings and Edges: Floors or walls having openings that aren't properly guarded.
  • Slippery or Obstructed Walkways: Includes wet, oily, or blocked paths leading to slips and trips.

Types of Fall Protection Equipment

There is a comprehensive selection of fall protection systems, each designed to address specific scenarios:

  • Full Body Harnesses: Full body harnesses are made to distribute fall forces across the body, keeping the worker upright. Harnesses today come with advanced features like padded straps, dorsal rings, and quick-release buckles. Harnesses are also offered in specialized variants, including flame-resistant, oil-repellent, and dielectric-safe models to suit specific industrial environments.
  • Lanyards: These are used to connect the harness to an anchor point. They are available in different types, including fall arrest, work positioning, restraint, and twin/forked. Lanyards are usually made of webbing, rope, or shock-absorbing materials, sometimes including adjustable lengths or energy absorbers for added safety.
  • Anchors and Anchorage Connectors: Anchors are secure attachment points for fall protection systems. These can either be permanent or temporary and are available in forms like cross-arm straps, beam clamps, door/window anchorages, and concrete D-rings. Proper placement and compatibility with other system components are essential.
  • Retractable Blocks: This safety equipment has an automatically retractable lifeline that adjusts with the workers’ movements. The blocks lock quickly in the event of a fall to reduce fall distance. These safety devices are ideal for dynamic work environments where mobility is essential.
  • Hooks and Karabiners: Safety Hooks are essential in ensuring secure attachment between system components. They are available in different locking mechanisms such as screw locking, double action locking, and triple action locking. They are primarily made from materials like stainless steel, alloy steel and aluminium, including specialised variants like dielectric hooks for electrical work.
  • Fall Protection Kits: These ready-to-use bundles include necessary components such as harnesses, lanyards, anchors, and sometimes rescue devices. They are designed for specific applications like tower climbing, confined space entry, or painting at heights.
  • Rope Access and Rescue Equipment: These are specialized equipment, including descenders, ascenders, pulleys, anchor lines, and rescue stretchers. They are used for accessing difficult-to-reach areas or executing rescues. Rope access and rescue equipment systems require trained personnel for effective use.
  • Load Arresters: These devices prevent the fall of heavy loads in the event of mechanical failure. They help protect both individuals and property. Most load arresters include shock-absorbing features and are often customised for the specific load and application.

How to Maintain Fall Protection Equipment to Ensure Workers’ Safety?

Inspecting and maintaining fall protection systems is essential to ensure their reliability.

  • Workers should inspect their equipment for signs of wear, fraying, corrosion, or any other damage.
  • Follow the manufacturer's guidelines for servicing and schedule maintenance timely.
  • Make sure you replace or revalidate equipment past its recommended expiry date or after a fall event.
  • Maintain logs of inspections for compliance and traceability.

At the same time, employers have a legal and ethical responsibility to ensure worker safety. They should follow these best practices:

  • Develop a fall protection plan customized to the worksite.
  • Conduct hazard assessments to identify fall risks during site planning.
  • Ensure all gear meets national and international standards.
  • Create a safe work culture by promoting open communication and employee participation in safety programs.
